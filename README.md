## The Business Problem

An automotive company with 25% EV market share in North America wants to enter India, where its current presence is under 2%. Before committing to a launch strategy, leadership needs answers to three questions:

- **Where** is the real opportunity — 2-wheelers or 4-wheelers?
- **Which states** offer the strongest entry markets?
- **When** is the right time to launch, and against whom?

---

## The Data

2M+ vehicle sales records across 30+ states and 20+ manufacturers, sourced from **[Vahan Sewa](https://vahan.parivahan.gov.in/)** — India's official vehicle registration portal — covering FY2022 to FY2024.

## What the Data Says

### The market is maturing — not stalling
India sold 2M+ EVs in 3 years, growing 4× in volume. Growth slowed from 186% to 31.5% year-on-year — a normal maturation curve. The more important number: **95 in every 100 vehicles sold is still non-electric.** The upside is enormous.

### 4-Wheelers are winning the value war
2-wheelers dominate volume (92.6% of units) but 4-wheelers generate **62% of total EV revenue** from just 7.4% of sales. The 4W segment grew at 116% CAGR vs 92% for 2W, and accelerated sharply in FY2024 (83% YoY vs 28% for 2W). A single 4W EV generates 17.6× the revenue of a 2W.

### The 2W market has already consolidated
OLA Electric grew from 14K units (FY2022) to 322K (FY2024) — 23× in two years. The top 3 reshuffled entirely between FY2023 and FY2024. Bottom performers are barely surviving. There is no viable entry point for a new 2W player.

### The 4W segment has no dominant player yet
Tata Motors leads with 48,181 units in FY2024 — strong, but far from a monopoly. Mahindra, MG Motor, and Hyundai are all growing fast. BYD India grew 566% CAGR (from just 33 units) — a watch signal for 3–5 years out, not today.

### Karnataka outperforms Delhi — without subsidies
Karnataka 2W penetration: **11.57%**. Delhi: 9.40% — despite Delhi offering ₹30,000 direct purchase subsidies and road tax waivers. Organic, demand-led adoption from Bengaluru's tech economy is more durable than incentive-driven adoption. Karnataka is the stronger strategic market.

### March peaks, June troughs — entry timing matters
March drives 291K units (fiscal year-end fleet purchases, subsidy deadlines). June drops to 107K — 63% below peak at monsoon onset. October–December form a festive secondary peak.

---

## Recommendations

**1. Enter the 4-Wheeler segment**
116% CAGR, 62% of total revenue, 153K total units sold across 3 years — the market is early and there is no dominant player. The 2W window has closed; the 4W window is open.

**2. Lead with Karnataka, scale into Maharashtra**
Karnataka offers organic demand with no subsidy dependence — the most durable market signal in the data. Maharashtra is India's largest absolute EV market with 10.5M EVs projected by 2030. Tamil Nadu and Kerala as Phase 2.

**3. Time the launch around market rhythms**
Build inventory and dealer network in July–August (monsoon low, minimal noise). Launch in October for festive season intent. Push fleet and corporate channels in February–March to capture the fiscal year-end surge. Avoid May–June entirely.
